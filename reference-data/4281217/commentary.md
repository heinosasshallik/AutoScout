## Key Finding

**Haiku misidentified the engine.** The car has a 1598cc/97kW engine which is the
1ZR-FAE (1.6L Valvematic). Haiku called it the 2ZR-FE (1.8L) and based its entire
research and scoring on the wrong engine. This inflated its scores — the 2ZR-FE is
a simpler, more reliable engine than the 1ZR-FAE.

Opus and Sonnet both correctly identified the 1ZR-FAE. Sonnet was more conservative
(MAYBE vs GO SEE IT), flagged more red flags (12 vs 8), and scored maintenance cost
lower (5 vs 7). Both approaches are defensible — Sonnet errs on the side of caution.

## Conclusion

- **Haiku**: Too unreliable for full evaluations. Engine misidentification is a dealbreaker.
- **Sonnet**: Correct and thorough at half the Opus cost. Good for production use.
- **Opus**: Best quality, most nuanced. Worth it for high-stakes evaluations.
