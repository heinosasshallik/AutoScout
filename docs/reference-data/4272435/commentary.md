## Key Finding

**All three models correctly identified the CVT and rejected the car.** Opus and Sonnet both
noted the important insight that the European 1.6L Corolla was *never offered* with a torque
converter automatic — only manual or CVT. Haiku identified it as CVT but was less specific
about the CVT variant name (just "CVT (Automatic)").

**Sonnet was the most aggressive scorer.** It gave mechanical reliability 4/10 and maintenance
cost 3/10 — much harsher than Opus (7/6) or Haiku (7/6). Sonnet's reasoning cited the 1ZR-FAE's
oil consumption, Valvematic failures, and the CVT's expensive repair costs, arriving at a
weighted score of 4.70. This is arguably *too* harsh on the engine itself (the 1ZR-FAE is
decent, the issue is the CVT), but it appropriately communicates that this is a bad buy.

**Haiku was the most generous.** Value for money at 7/10 and seller trustworthiness at 7/10
seem overly positive for a car that fails a hard requirement.

## Conclusion

- **Opus**: Correct verdict with balanced scores. Good insight about the European Corolla's
  transmission options.
- **Sonnet**: Correct verdict, most conservative. Potentially over-penalized the engine to
  make the point, but the low 4.70 weighted score clearly communicates "don't bother."
- **Haiku**: Correct verdict but scores too generous. The 6.45 weighted score is higher than
  some legitimately viable MAYBE cars.
