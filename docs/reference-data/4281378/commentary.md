## Key Finding

**All three models misidentified the engine.** The listing shows 1794cc and 95kW. This does
not match any 2ZR variant:
- The 2ZR-FE has **1798cc** (bore 80.5 × stroke 88.3), not 1794cc, and produces 98-103kW.
- The 2ZR-FAE has **1798cc** and produces 108kW.

The engine with exactly **1794cc** (bore 79.0 × stroke 91.5) and **95kW** is the **1ZZ-FE** —
a completely different, older engine family (ZZ series). The 1ZZ-FE was used in the
**Avensis T250 (2nd generation, 2003-2008)**, not the T270. A "2009 Avensis" with 1794cc
and 95kW is a late-production T250, not a T270. The T270 (launched January 2009) only
used the 2ZR-FAE (1798cc, 108kW) for its 1.8L petrol option.

**Opus (2ZR-FE)** got the wrong engine family entirely. Sonnet and Haiku (2ZR-FAE) also got
the wrong family — and the wrong power output (the 2ZR-FAE produces 108kW, not 95kW).

**Impact on evaluations:** The 1ZZ-FE is notorious for severe oil consumption due to
undersized piston rings — a much worse and better-documented problem than what any 2ZR
variant has. Toyota even issued warranty extensions for 1ZZ-FE oil consumption. At 270k km,
this is a serious concern that all three evaluations underweighted. Additionally, the car
is a T250 (older generation), which has different safety ratings, different interior, and
different known issues than the T270 that all three models assumed.

All three models also claimed the car had a timing chain, which happens to be correct —
the 1ZZ-FE does use a timing chain. But the 1ZZ-FE's chain tensioner has different failure
characteristics than the 2ZR's.

**Opus was harsh on seller trustworthiness.** Opus gave 3/10 — the lowest score in any
category for any model in this comparison — flagging the zero-effort description and no phone
number. Sonnet and Haiku both gave 7/10 for the same seller. This is a meaningful calibration
difference: Opus weighed the *absence* of information as a red flag at 270k km, while
Sonnet/Haiku treated a feature-list description as adequate.

**Haiku inflated scores across the board.** Value 7/10, cosmetic 7/10, safety 9/10, and
seller trust 7/10 are all the highest of the three models, producing a 7.10 weighted score
that seems optimistic for a 270k km car with no service history.

## Conclusion

- **Opus**: Wrong engine family (2ZR-FE instead of 1ZZ-FE). Got the generation wrong too
  (T270 vs T250). Still the most conservative scorer and appropriately harsh on seller trust.
- **Sonnet**: Wrong engine family (2ZR-FAE instead of 1ZZ-FE). Flagged Valvematic risks
  that don't apply, but maintenance caution (4/10) was directionally right since the
  1ZZ-FE has even worse oil consumption issues.
- **Haiku**: Wrong engine family (2ZR-FAE instead of 1ZZ-FE). Scores too generous across
  the board. The 7.10 weighted score doesn't adequately communicate the risk of a 270k km
  car with no service history.
